# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Sales and account managers at small-to-mid-size businesses. They work inside this tool for hours at a stretch — following up on quotations, tracking customer relationships, reviewing AI-drafted emails before sending. They are commercially driven and time-pressured; the interface needs to surface the right signal fast and then stay out of the way.

## Product Purpose

An AI-powered customer communication assistant layered on top of Microsoft 365. It connects Outlook email to a CRM layer that tracks customers, generates draft replies for human approval, monitors follow-up deadlines, and surfaces customer history through a chatbot. The goal: reduce the mental overhead of managing customer relationships so sales staff can focus on the conversation, not the admin.

## Brand Personality

Ambitious, bold, premium. This is the tool a sales team shows off to peers — not because it's flashy, but because it is visibly more considered than everything else they've used. Calm surface, decisive choices underneath.

## Anti-references

- Google Workspace: utilitarian gray, anonymous, no identity of its own
- HubSpot / Salesforce / Zoho: enterprise-CRM clutter, gamification badges, pixel-packed sidebars, hero-metric dashboards
- Generic SaaS dashboards: identical card grids, gradient-text callouts, purple-to-blue hero sections

## Design Principles

1. **Craft is the product.** The interface is itself a competitive advantage. Every screen should feel authored, not assembled. Apple and Craft earn trust before a user reads a word.
2. **Calm authority.** Focused and calm is not the same as timid. Use restraint to project confidence: white space is the decision, not the absence of one.
3. **Signal before context.** Sales professionals need to know what requires action right now. Surface the urgent thing first; let everything else recede.
4. **Premium through precision.** Premium is not decoration added at the end. It is the right detail in the right place, and nothing extraneous anywhere.
5. **Every interaction is intentional.** Hover states, transitions, empty states, and error messages are all part of the product. None of them should feel default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. 4.5:1 contrast on body text, 3:1 on large text and interactive elements. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` where animations are used.
